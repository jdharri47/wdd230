Vast History
Vernal has a large and diverse history. Unlike most towns and cities in Utah, Vernal was not settled by Mormon Pioneers. It began when President Abraham Lincoln set the Uinta Basin as the Uintah Indian Reservation, appointing Captain Pardon Dodds Indian agent. Dodds moved to the Ashley Valley when he retired building the first cabin in the valley. The town got its true start in 1879 when a fort was built in the Ashley Valley. The town that grew out from the fort became known as Ashley Center however was changed to Vernal when the postal service came to the town because the name of Ashley was already taken.


Dinosaurs?
Being in the center of what is called Dinosaur land, Vernal is well known for its dinosaur museum and its proximity to Dinosaur National Monument.

Where is Vernal?
Vernal is on the northeastern corner of Utah. Lying within the Uintah Basin, Vernal is surrounded by mountains on all sides. With a population of roughly 10,000 people, Vernal is the largest city in the Uintah Basin and is also the seat of the Uintah County.

Things to do
There are many things to do in Vernal. There are many hiking/biking trails, as well as, camp sites, fishing spots, and of course the dinosaur museum and The Dinosaur National Monument
